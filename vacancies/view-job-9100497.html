Job Description:
GENERAL SUMMARY/ OVERVIEW STATEMENT: Summarize the nature and level of work performed.


The Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) and the Corrigan Minehan Heart Center are seeking a highly motivated Clinical Research Data Analyst and Manager interested in helping curate, organize, and analyze large-scale multimodal electronic health record data for a novel research initiative. The objectives of the research are to leverage machine learning and artificial intelligence to determine the causes of and identify individuals at risk for cardiovascular disease. Insights from this initiative are ultimately expected to lead to improvements in health outcomes.
The candidate will work with a team of investigators at MGH and the Broad Institute including physician scientists, computational biologists, epidemiologists, statisticians, bioinformaticians, computer scientists, and machine learning engineers. The candidate will work with electronic health record data spanning a research repository containing up to 6 million records. Fields will span diagnostic, medication, notes, laboratory, electrocardiographic, imaging, and genomic data.
Ideal candidates will have e xpertise in clinical informatics and database management, as well as data analysis, and will be highly motivated and eager to learn about cardiovascular disease. Experience with genetics would be advantageous but is not required. Exceptional organizational and interpersonal skills, attention to detail, and the ability to prioritize multiple tasks are essential. The candidate must be self-motivated, independent in task completion, and comfortable accepting feedback and guidance from colleagues. A high level of discipline and adherence to confidentiality is essential, as the analyst is given access to personal and confidential medical information.

PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: Indicate key areas of responsibility, major job duties, special projects and key objectives for this position. These items should be evaluated throughout the year and included in the written annual evaluation.


Specific responsibilities will include the following:

  • Provide clinical data analysis and management support to the investigative team.

  • Curate and organize electronic medical record data from multiple sources and repositories.

  • Create and maintain longitudinal health record and curated disease-specific datasets.

  • Perform data analysis against hospital systems to glean meaningful insights and produce actionable reports for team.

  • Develop and implement electronic phenotyping algorithms for electronic health record data.

  • Apply traditional and novel epidemiologic and statistical methods to identify disease risk factors and analyze relations between risk factors and health outcomes.

  • Work with research information security and IRB to ensure compliance and adherence to institutional, federal, and other security, regulatory, and compliance policies relating to health data management.

  • Participate in the development of clinical research documents (eg. Protocols, Case Report Forms, Reports and Statistical Analysis Plans) where relevant.

  • Develop a Data Management Plan (DMP) for studies, maintain DMP throughout lifecycle of study projects, and ensure DMP is followed according to study design and requirements.

  • Develop database data specifications, including user requirements, edit rules/checks, query logic and data validations.

  • Work with investigative team and Principal Investigator to support, maintain and further develop our analytical capabilities which includes but is not limited to dashboard and report development, QA of existing reports, and documentation of system functionality.

  • Work with project manager to ensure data access is provisioned in accordance with protocol approvals.

  • Assist and provide input into study and project level data analysis plan.

  • Collaborate with IT and implementation team(s) to address Clinical application requests and/or changes to Clinical database systems.

  • Participate within a team of scientists to foster a culture of scientific excellence.

  • Work with coinvestigators, postdoctoral fellows, research analysts, research nurses, and other team members to execute approved protocols.
