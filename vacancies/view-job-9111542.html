GENERAL SUMMARY/ OVERVIEW STATEMENT: Summarize the nature and level of work performed.


The Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) and the Corrigan Minehan Heart Center are seeking exceptional candidates to join the lab of Dr. Steven Lubitz in the Cardiovascular Research Center, serving in the role of an electronic health record research analyst. The candidate will join an interdisciplinary team of investigators at MGH and the Broad Institute including epidemiologists, senior statisticians, and clinicians, computational biologists, bioinformaticians, clinical informatics personnel, computer scientists, machine learning engineers , research coordinators, and research nurses. Projects in the lab are focused on 1) identifying factors related to cardiovascular disease, 2) modeling strategies to improve health outcomes, and 3) implementing pragmatic interventions and mobile technology to diagnose cardiovascular conditions and minimize morbidity. Tasks will focus on helping curate, organize, and analyze large-scale multimodal electronic health record data. Insights from this work are ultimately expected to lead to improvements in health outcomes.
The candidate will work with electronic health record data spanning a research repository containing up to 6 million records including a growing biorepository of over 100K individuals with DNA samples . Fields will span diagnostic, procedural, medication, notes, laboratory, electrocardiographic, imaging, and genomic data.
The candidate will be a critical member of the lab organization. Primary roles will include the development, validation, and maintenance of electronic phenotyping algorithms; and the development of computational scripts for database analysis. Tasks will include working with health metadata (e.g., ICD9/10 codes, CPT codes), extracting features from notes and reports using regular expression syntax and natural language processing, and comparing feature algorithms using statistical methods.
Ideal candidates will have e xpertise in health or clinical informatics as well as data analysis, and will be highly motivated and eager to learn about cardiovascular disease. Exceptional organizational and interpersonal skills, attention to detail, and the ability to prioritize multiple tasks are essential. The candidate must be self-motivated, independent in task completion, and comfortable accepting feedback and guidance from colleagues. A high level of discipline and adherence to confidentiality is essential, as the analyst is given access to personal and confidential medical information.
This position is ideal for either individuals with a Master of Science degree in health or medical informatics, computer science, biostatistics, epidemiology, bioinformatics, or public health. Exceptional candidates without advanced degrees will be considered. Prior experience with electronic health records or claims data is preferred.


PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES: Indicate key areas of responsibility, major job duties, special projects and key objectives for this position. These items should be evaluated throughout the year and included in the written annual evaluation.


Specific responsibilities will include the following:

  • Develop and implement electronic phenotyping algorithms for electronic health record data.

  • Statistically compare electronic phenotyping algorithm performance and iteratively modify.

  • Participate in the development of clinical research documents (eg. Protocols, Case Report Forms, Reports and Statistical Analysis Plans) where relevant.

  • Work with investigative team and Principal Investigator to support, maintain and further develop our analytical capabilities which includes but not limited to dashboard and report development, QA of existing reports, and documentation of system functionality.

  • Provide clinical data analysis and management support to the investigative team.

  • Work with project manager to ensure data access is provisioned in accordance with protocol approvals.

  • Participate within a team of scientists to foster a culture of scientific excellence.

  • Work with coinvestigators, postdoctoral fellows, research analysts, research nurses, and other team members to execute approved protocols.
